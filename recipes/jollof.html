Description

Jollof rice is a vibrant, flavorful West African one-pot dish featuring long-grain rice cooked in a rich tomato, chili, and onion sauce, seasoned with spices like garlic, thyme, and ginger, resulting in a signature reddish hue and deeply savory taste, often served at celebrations with meat, fish, or vegetables. It's a cultural emblem, with variations across countries like Nigeria, Ghana, and Senegal, sparking friendly "Jollof Wars" over who makes the best version. Key Characteristics: Base: A blended puree of tomatoes, red bell peppers, onions, and hot peppers (like Scotch Bonnet), creating a sweet and spicy foundation. Rice: Long-grain rice, often parboiled, absorbs the sauce for maximum flavor. Color: Deep red or orange, from the tomato base and sometimes paprika. Spices: Aromatic blend including garlic, ginger, thyme, and bay leaves, adding earthy and warm notes. One-Pot Meal: All ingredients are cooked together, allowing flavors to meld beautifully. Cultural Significance: A staple for gatherings, symbolizing identity and pride, with unique regional twists.

Steps

  1. Prepare the dough:
      In a bowl, mix flour, yeast, salt, warm water, and a little olive oil. Knead the mixture until it forms a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest in a warm place for about 1 hour, or until it doubles in size.
  2. Preheat the oven:
      Preheat your oven to 220–250°C (425–480°F). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven while it heats.
  3. Shape the dough:
      Punch down the risen dough and roll it out on a floured surface into a round pizza base. Transfer it to a baking tray or pizza pan.
  4. Add the sauce:
      Spread a thin, even layer of tomato pizza sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  5. Add cheese:
      Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese generously over the sauce.
  6. Add pepperoni:
      Arrange pepperoni slices evenly on top of the cheese.
  7. Bake the pizza:
      Place the pizza in the preheated oven and bake for 10–15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Serve:
      Remove from the oven, let it cool slightly, slice, and enjoy your homemade pepperoni pizza!
